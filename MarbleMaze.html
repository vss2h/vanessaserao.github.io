3D printed Marble Machine

Introduction and Process

The 3d printed marble maze is a project made for a Biology class assignment.
The requirement for this assignement was creating a marble machine that would be at least five-feet tall, keep a marble in motion for 8 seconds to a minute, be under $10. The assignment allowed students to be creative.

Not having much biological knowledge, I used my technical knowledge to come up with idea for my marble machine.
My approach was 3d printing every part that was needed to build the maze.The process took a month, including printing and building.

Not knowing how to design 3d models, I was able to find resources that other people invented on Thingiverse and rearrange it to fit my needs and design the maze.

building process

Since 3d printing was free on campus, I only had to buy a few dowels, screws, which cost me $7 total.
Today, the project is displayed at the MakerSpace of the Library and hopefully inspire students to expand their creativity.
